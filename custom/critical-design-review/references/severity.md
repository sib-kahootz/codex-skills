# Objection Severity

Use these levels to rank objections.

- `Blocker`: The proposal should not proceed as written. It has a likely correctness, security, data, migration, compliance, or operational failure, or it contradicts a hard requirement.
- `Major`: The proposal may proceed only after the objection is resolved or explicitly accepted by the right owner. It creates meaningful user, maintenance, delivery, rollback, or support risk.
- `Moderate`: The proposal has a weakness that should be addressed before implementation finishes, but it is unlikely to invalidate the whole direction by itself.
- `Minor`: The proposal has a small clarity, evidence, or polish gap. Report sparingly; avoid filling the memo with low-leverage objections.

Prefer fewer, sharper objections over exhaustive lists. Do not inflate severity to sound adversarial; the force should come from the evidence.
