# Example personas

These example personas are based on a secure cloud collaboration platform for cross-organisation workspaces, document sharing, forums, surveys, calendars, task management, extranets, and partner portals.

## Paula - Public sector programme manager

Context: Manages multi-organisation projects involving internal teams, suppliers, and external stakeholders.
Goals: Keep project documents, actions, meetings, and decisions in one secure place.
Pain points: Long email chains, unclear document ownership, duplicated files, missed actions.
Constraints: Needs strong permissions, auditability, low onboarding friction, and confidence around security.
Preferences: Simple workspace setup, clear dashboards, task visibility, document versioning, and structured discussions.
Objections: Will resist anything that feels like "another tool" or requires heavy IT support.
Tone: Practical, time-poor, risk-aware.

## Imran - Information governance lead

Context: Reviews collaboration tools before they are approved for sensitive organisational use.
Goals: Ensure external collaboration is secure, compliant, auditable, and controllable.
Pain points: Shadow IT, uncontrolled file sharing, unclear access rights, weak audit trails.
Constraints: Needs evidence of security controls, access management, encryption, and policy alignment.
Preferences: Granular permissions, audit logs, secure file sharing, clear admin controls.
Objections: Will push back on vague security claims or features that increase data leakage risk.
Tone: Cautious, precise, evidence-led.

## Sophie - Supplier account manager

Context: Works with clients through shared portals, uploading documents, responding to comments, and tracking actions.
Goals: Find the right information quickly and collaborate without chasing emails.
Pain points: Unclear where documents live, repeated requests for updates, difficult client-side tools.
Constraints: Has limited time to learn client systems and may use multiple portals across customers.
Preferences: Email notifications, easy document upload, clear tasks, simple permissions, browser-based access.
Objections: Will disengage if the workspace feels cluttered or hard to navigate.
Tone: Direct, commercially minded, low patience for admin.

## Helen - Membership or community manager

Context: Runs online communities, working groups, consultations, or member collaboration spaces.
Goals: Encourage participation, gather feedback, share resources, and keep members informed.
Pain points: Low engagement, scattered feedback, difficult reporting, members missing updates.
Constraints: Needs tools that work for non-technical users with varied engagement levels.
Preferences: Forums, surveys, notifications, dashboards, document areas, and simple reporting.
Objections: Will worry if the experience feels too corporate or complex for occasional users.
Tone: Inclusive, facilitative, outcome-focused.

## David - IT operations manager

Context: Supports deployment and ongoing administration of collaboration tools across teams and partners.
Goals: Reduce support burden while giving departments controlled autonomy.
Pain points: Bespoke requests, permission mistakes, user confusion, tools that need constant admin intervention.
Constraints: Needs predictable licensing, browser-based access, minimal local software dependency, and manageable governance.
Preferences: Delegated workspace management, reusable patterns, clear admin controls, supportable integrations.
Objections: Will resist features that create hidden complexity or increase support tickets.
Tone: Pragmatic, systems-minded, skeptical of unnecessary complexity.
